--- title: "Dr. Durant: CS-321 Computer Graphics: Oral Presentations (Fall 2004)" ---

This assignment counts for 10% of your quarter grade. You only have 5 minutes to convince the class that you are knowledgeable on the subject you choose to present, so be sure to adequately prioritize this assignment.

Overview

As part of the course, each student will make a brief (five minute) technical presentation on some subject related to computer graphics. Some possible subjects are:

  • A graphics rendering algorithm.
  • A hardware component of a graphics system.
  • A programming technique found useful in the lab.
  • A new application of computer graphics.

This list is meant to stimulate ideas, not to limit the range of topics.

Guidelines

  • You must submit your topic to the instructor by 4:00 P.M. on Monday of week 6.
  • Presentations will be scheduled at the beginning of the class period. Therefore, presenters and other class members must be prompt.
  • Presentations are limited to five minutes in duration. Presenters exceeding this limit risk being unceremoniously cut off. A short period for questions and comments from class members will be provided and is not included in the five minute limit.
  • Each presenter should arrange for any necessary audio-visual equipment. The instructor will consult on this process if needed.
  • Grading will be based on content, clarity, relation of topic to computer graphics, appropriate use of audio-visual equipment, ability to answer questions, and appropriate use of time. (Evaluation sheet)
  • You should take this assignment seriously as it accounts for 10% of your quarter grade. Students who do not devote adequate time to researching and preparing their presentation should expect to receive a poor grade.
